Visitor Analysis and Persona

The Verge

URL: theverge.com

Visitor Analysis

The demographics for The Verge are mostly young men who are interested in technology. They are probably between teen and mid-thirties year old. Most of the visitors are probably students or currently work in the tech industry. The audience is interested in computers and most likely lives in a wealthier area, or probably not farming area.

The site is very easy to use and is very clear. The homepage has general stories in many topics. The top right corner has all the new articles listed and the top left corner brings you to a list of topics. If you click on a topic it will pull up articles and stories just about that topic. The site is clear and easy to navigate. It made the mobile experience enjoyable because things were hidden if they were not needed and they only included one story per row. This allows each story block to be large and fill the width of the phone. If they had tried to put two or three stories on each row it would have been harder to read.

This site looks like it was custom made for mobile experience and specifically my phone. It looks neat and clean. It automatically adjusted on different size screens as well.

Persona

Name: John McNerd
Age: 25
Gender: Male
Highest Level of Education: bachelors degree in Computer Science

John McNerd works currently as a computer programmer for a software company. He likes to come to theverge.com to hear about new technologies and new products that are coming out. He likes to stay up to date with technology and learn more. He discusses technology with his coworkers and always likes to stay up to date with the latest things. He sometimes goes to the news or other websites for information as well. He usually accesses this site after work to relax.

John has all of the coolest tech, so he regularly uses his smartphone, tablet or laptop to view his content. he regularly uses news apps and regular phone utility apps. John usually browses the web for 2 hours a day for personal use. About 75% of that time is in a mobile platform.

John McNerd is motivated through technology and by knowing and learning new things. He always likes to discuss things he learned about with his coworkers. He wants to receive good clear content that is easy to understand and always stays up to date.

Site Contrast

Real Clear Politics

URL: realclearpolitics.com

Did they or did they not know who their target audience was? Yes they knew who their target audience is, people who follow the news and politics.
Did they or did they not meet the needs of their audience? They meet the needs, but not in a very pretty way. It is very rough around the edges. A functional site, but not very pretty.
Did they or did they not organize content to facilitate it being found? Some of the content is organized, but no too easy to find yourself around.
Did the site facilitate visitors in a mobile environment? It is better than being in a desktop website, but it is not a very good mobile site. It is almost entirely text with only ads as pictures. The alignment is also weird.
